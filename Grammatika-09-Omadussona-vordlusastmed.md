# 9 · Adjektiiv: komparatsioon. Omadussõna: võrdlusastmete moodustamine. Adjective: Degrees of Comparison

Chapter 9 of the *Eesti keele grammatika* reference, **book pages 32–33**. The three degrees of comparison for Estonian adjectives — positive, comparative, and superlative — and how each is formed.

> Estonian first, English alongside. Word forms are **positive : genitive > comparative**, unless noted otherwise.

**On this page:** [Positiiv](#1-positiiv-e-algvõrre--positive) · [Komparatiiv](#2-komparatiiv-e-keskvõrre--comparative) · [Superlatiiv](#3-superlatiiv-e-ülivõrre--superlative)

---

Similarly to nouns, adjectives inflect for case and number in Estonian. Adjectives have three degrees of comparison — the **positive**, **comparative**, and **superlative** degrees.

## 1. Positiiv e algvõrre · Positive

Adjectives in the positive degree have **no characteristic endings or markers** in Estonian.

Adjectives functioning as attributes **precede the noun** and **agree** with the noun in case and number, except for the last four cases (see [Chapter 7](Grammatika-07-Ulejaanud-kaanded): translative, terminative, essive, comitative, abessive).

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Mööda teed tuli **vana** naine. | Along the road came an **old** lady. |
| **Vanal** naisel ei ole lapsi. | The **old** lady has no kids. |

---

## 2. Komparatiiv e keskvõrre · Comparative

The comparative is usually formed from the **genitive stem** of the positive degree by adding the marker **-m**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| suur : suure > suure**m** | large > larger |

In some two-syllable adjectives with the stem ending **-a** or **-u**, the stem vowel becomes **-e**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| vana : vana > van**em** | old > older |
| paks : paksu > paks**em** | thick > thicker |

There are some **irregular** adjectives, which form the comparative from a different (shortened) stem:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| hea : parem | good : better |
| lühike : lühem | short : shorter |
| pisike : pisem | small : smaller |

The comparative form is **declinable**, and the stem ends in **-a** in the genitive:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| raskem : raskema : raskemat | heavier |

---

## 3. Superlatiiv e ülivõrre · Superlative

The superlative is formed in **two ways**:

**a)** the adverb **kõige** is added to the comparative form of the adjective. This form of the superlative can be formed with **all** adjectives:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| uus : uuem > kõige uuem | new : newer > newest |
| rõõmus : rõõmsam > kõige rõõmsam | happy : happier > happiest |
| hea : parem > kõige parem | good : better > best |

**b)** The **short form** of the superlative is formed with the marker **-im**, without *kõige*. The short superlative is **less common** than the long form:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| ilus, ilusam > ilus**aim** | prettiest |
| hele, heledam > heled**aim** | lightest |

Like the comparative form, the superlative can be **declined**.
