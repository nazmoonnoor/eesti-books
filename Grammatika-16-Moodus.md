# 16 · Moodus. Indikatiiv, konditsionaal, kvotatiiv ja imperatiiv. Moods. The Indicative, Conditional, Quotative and Imperative

Chapter 16 of the *Eesti keele grammatika* reference, **book pages 57–60**. Estonian's four moods and how the object case of a singular noun shifts between the indicative and the imperative.

> Estonian first, English alongside.

Estonian has **four moods**: the indicative, conditional, imperative, and quotative. The moods are distinguished by mood markers.

## 1. Indikatiiv e kindel kõneviis · Indicative Mood

The indicative has **no marker**. The 1st person present indicative is the basic verb form, which is used to form all the other present indicative forms (see [Chapter 13: Basic Verb Forms](Grammatika-13-Verbi-pohivormid)). The imperfect indicative is usually formed by adding the marker **-si/-i** to the -ma infinitive stem (see [Chapter 15: Past Tenses](Grammatika-15-Verbi-minevikuvormid)).

The perfect form is composed of the present form of the verb **olema** and the **-nud** participle of the verb (see ibid.). The pluperfect is composed of the imperfect of the verb **olema** and the **-nud** participle of the verb (see ibid.).

---

## 2. Konditsionaal e tingiv kõneviis · Conditional Mood

The conditional mood has **present** and **perfect** forms.

The **present conditional** is formed from the **1st person present indicative** stem by adding the marker **-ks** and the personal ending, which is **optional**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| õppima, õppida, õpin > õpiksin | study > I would study |

| Eesti keeles | English |
|---|---|
| ma õpiks/in ~ õpi/ks | I would study |
| sa õpi/ks/id ~ õpi/ks | you would study |
| ta õpi/ks | he/she would study |
| me õpi/ks/ime ~ õpi/ks | we would study |
| te õpi/ks/ite ~ õpi/ks | you (pl) would study |
| nad õpi/ks/id ~ õpi/ks | they would study |

The negative form of the present conditional: **ei + present stem + -ks**.

The conditional expresses an action that will take place **only under certain conditions**.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Kui ma **saaksin** rikkaks, **ostaksin** endale jahi. | If I became rich, I would buy myself a yacht. |

The conditional can also express a **polite request, wish, or suggestion**.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ma **tahaksin** jõuluks arvutit. | I would like to get a computer for Christmas. |

The **perfect conditional** is composed of the verb **olema** with the conditional marker (**-ks**) and the **-nud** participle of the verb.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| õppima, õppida, õpin > ma õpiksin, ma oleks(in) õppinud, sa oleks(id) õppinud, ta oleks õppinud, etc. | I would study, I would have studied, you would have studied, s/he would have studied |

The negative form of the perfect conditional: **ei + oleks + -nud** participle.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Kui ma **oleks teadnud**, et sa oled haige, siis ma **ei oleks** sind kodus **oodanud**. | If I had known that you were ill, I wouldn't have waited for you at home. |

---

## 3. Kvotatiiv e kaudne kõneviis · Quotative Mood

The quotative has two tenses — the **present** and the **perfect**. The present is formed by adding the marker **-vat** to the **-ma infinitive** stem. There are **no personal endings**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| õppi/ma, õppida, õpin > ma, sa, ta, me, te, nad õppi/vat | to study > am/are/is reported to study |

The quotative indicates an action or a situation about the reality of which the speaker is **not certain**. The quotative is often used to convey information received from a **third party**.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Vanaema **olevat** haige. | Grandmother **is said to be** ill. |

The **perfect form of the quotative** is formed similarly to the perfect indicative and conditional — the verb **olema** with the quotative marker **-vat** plus the **-nud** participle of the verb.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| õppima > ma olevat õppi/nud | to study > I am said to have studied |

| Eesti keeles | English |
|---|---|
| ma, sa, ta, me, te, nad olevat õppi/nud | I, you, he/she, we, you, they are said to have studied |

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ta **olevat** kuulsaks **saanud**. | S/he **is reported to have become** famous. |

The negative form of the perfect quotative: **ei + olevat + -nud** participle.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Me **ei olevat** temaga varem **kohtunud**. | We **are reported not to have met** him/her before. |

## 4. Imperatiiv e käskiv kõneviis · Imperative Mood

The imperative mood expresses an **order or a request**. The imperative has **only the present form**.

The **2nd person plural** imperative forms are formed from the **-da infinitive** stem by adding the marker **-ge/ke**.

The marker **-ge** is added to the -da infinitive stem with the marker **-da**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| tõlkima : tõlkida : tõlgin > tõlki**ge**! | to translate > you (pl) translate! |

The marker **-ke** is added to the -da infinitive stem with the marker **-ta**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| vaatama, vaadata : vaatan > vaada**ke**! | to look > you (pl) look! |

The marker **-ge** is added to the -da infinitive stem with the marker **-a** if -a is preceded by a **vowel or a voiced consonant**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| käima, käia, käin > käi**ge**! | to walk > you (pl) walk! |
| tulema, tulla, tulen > tul**ge**! | to come > you (pl) come! |

The marker **-ke** is added to the -da infinitive stem with the marker **-a** if -a is preceded by **-d, -t, -h**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| andma, anda, annan > and**ke**! | to give > you (pl) give! |
| tegema, teha, teen > teh**ke**! | to do > you (pl) do! |

The **2nd person singular** imperative is formed from the stem of the **1st person present indicative**; **no marker** is added to the stem.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| hakka/n > hakka! | begin > you (sg) begin! |
| õpi/n > õpi! | study > you (sg) study! |

In the case of **one-syllable verbs ending in -oo or -öö**, the formation of the imperative is **irregular**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| jooma, juua, joon > joo! jooge! | to drink > you drink! |
| sööma, süüa, söön > söö! sööge! | to eat > you eat! |

The negative form of the imperative: **ära/ärge + imperative form**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| õpi! > ära õpi! õppige! > ärge õppige! | you (sg) study! don't study! you (pl) study! don't study! |

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| **Istu** siia! | Sit here! |
| **Enne mõtle**, siis **ütle**! | Think twice before you say something! |
| **Tulge** sisse! | Come in! |
| **Ütelge** veel kord! | Say it again! |
| **Ära räägi** nii kõvasti! | Don't talk so loud! |
| **Ärge** mind **oodake**! | Don't wait for me! |

**Relation of the object case (sg) to the mood of a verb:**

| Affirmative form of the indicative + genitive/partitive | Affirmative form of the imperative + nominative/partitive |
|---|---|
| Ma avan **ukse**. (gen.) *I will open the door.* | **Ava uks**! (nominative) *Open the door!* |
| Ma kuulan **muusikat**. (part.) *I'm listening to the music.* | **Kuula muusikat**! (partitive) *Listen to the music!* |

| Negative form + partitive | Negative form + partitive |
|---|---|
| Ma ei kirjuta **harjutust**. (partitive) *I'm not doing the written exercise.* | **Ära kirjuta harjutust**! (partitive) *Don't do the written exercise!* |

The plural forms of the object cases — see [Partitive Plural, Chapter 8](Grammatika-08-Mitmuse-osastav).

## 5. Kõneviiside kokkuvõte · Summary of Moods

**Moods** — *lugema "to read"*

| Moods | Present | Imperfect | Perfect | Pluperfect |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Indicative | ma loen *I'm reading* | lugesin *I read* | olen lugenud *I have read* | olin lugenud *I had read* |
| Conditional | ma loeksin *I would read* | – | oleksin lugenud *I would have read* | – |
| Quotative | ma lugevat *I'm said to read* | – | olevat lugenud *I'm said to have read* | – |
| Imperative | te lugege! *you (pl) read!* | – | – | – |
