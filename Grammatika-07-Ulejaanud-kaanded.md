# 7 · Saav, rajav, olev, ilmaütlev, kaasaütlev kääne. Translative, Terminative, Essive, Abessive, Comitative

Chapter 7 of the *Eesti keele grammatika* reference, **book pages 25–27**. Five more cases, all formed from the genitive stem: the translative (transformation/purpose), terminative (a limit), essive (a function or feature), abessive (lack of something), and comitative (means or company).

> Estonian first, English alongside. Word forms are **nominative : genitive : partitive > case form**.

## 1. Translatiiv e saav · Translative

**kelleks?** *into whom?*, **milleks?** *into what?*

The translative is formed from the **genitive stem** by adding the case ending **-ks**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| aeg : aja : aega > aja**ks** | by (this) time |
| tütar : tütre : tütart > tütre**ks** | (into) a daughter |

**The translative is used:**

1. to express **transformation** into someone or something — *Ta saab **õpetajaks**.* He will become **a teacher**.
2. to express **purpose** — *Vajame **elamiseks** ruumi.* We need a room **for living**.
3. to express a **period of time** — *Tule **paariks tunniks** meile.* Come over to our place **for a couple of hours**.
4. to express a **temporal limit** — *Too raamat **homseks**.* Bring the book **by tomorrow**.

Verbs that take the translative: osutuma *turn out to be*, saama *become*, purunema *break (into pieces)*, valmistuma *prepare (for sth)*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ta valmistus **eksamiks**. | He was preparing **for an exam**. |
| Vaas purunes **kildudeks**. | The vase broke **into pieces**. |

## 2. Terminatiiv e rajav · Terminative

**kelleni?** *as far as whom?*, **milleni?** *as far as what?*

The terminative is formed from the **genitive stem** by adding the case ending **-ni**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| jõgi : jõe : jõge > jõe**ni** | as far as the river |
| õhtu : õhtu : õhtut > õhtu**ni** | till evening |

**The terminative is used:**

1. to express a **spatial limit** — *Ta jooksis kõrge **puuni** ja tagasi.* He ran **as far as the** tall **tree** and back.
2. to express a **temporal limit** — *Nad on hommikust **õhtuni** tööl.* They work from morning **till night**.

Some verbs that take the terminative: küündima *reach*, ulatuma *extend*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Puu ulatus maja **katuseni**. | The tree reached from the house **until the roof**. |

## 3. Essiiv e olev · Essive

**kellena?** *as who?*, **millena?** *as what?*

The essive is formed from the **genitive stem** by adding the case ending **-na**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| valge : valge : valget > valge**na** | as white |
| kokk : koka : kokka > koka**na** | as a cook |
| laps : lapse : last > lapse**na** | as a child |

**The essive is used:**

1. to express a **function or a feature** — *Ta töötab restoranis **nõudepesijana**.* She works **as a dishwasher** at a restaurant.
2. for **comparison** — *See range ülemus on **inimesena** sümpaatne.* This strict boss is pleasant **as a person**.

Some verbs that require the essive: näima *look, seem*, tunduma *seem*, töötama *work (as)*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| See mees töötab **kokana**. | This man works **as a chef**. |

## 4. Abessiiv e ilmaütlev · Abessive

**kelleta?** *without whom?*, **milleta?** *without what?*

The abessive is formed from the **genitive stem** by adding the case ending **-ta**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| nimi : nime : nime > nime**ta** | without a name |
| mees : mehe : meest > mehe**ta** | without a man |

**The abessive is used** to express **lack of something**.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| **Rahata** ei saa autot osta. | One cannot buy a car **without money**. |

## 5. Komitatiiv e kaasaütlev · Comitative

**kellega?** *with whom?*, **millega?** *with what?*

The comitative is formed from the **genitive stem** by adding the case ending **-ga**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| sõber : sõbra : sõpra > sõbra**ga** | with a friend |

**The comitative is used:**

1. to express a **means of action** — *Ta läheb tööle **bussiga**.* He goes to work **by bus**.
2. to express **companionship** — *Te lähete koos **sõbraga** välja.* You're going out **with your friend**.
3. to denote a **period of time** — *Ta kirjutas esse **kolme tunniga**.* He wrote the essay **in three hours**.
4. to denote a **feature** — *Kõrge **kontsaga** kingad polnud mugavad.* Shoes **with** high **heels** were uncomfortable.

Below are some verbs that take the comitative: abielluma *to get married*, juhtuma *to happen*, kattuma *to be covered (with)*, kauplema *to trade (in)*, kohtuma *to meet*, konsulteerima *to consult*, leppima *to put up with*, pahandama *to be angry*, piirduma *to be confined to*, piirnema *to border*, rahulduma *to be satisfied*, riidlema *to scold*, rääkima *to speak*, sarnanema *to resemble*, seostuma *to be associated with*, suhtlema *to communicate*, sõbrustama *to make friends (with sb)*, tegelema *to deal (with)*, tutvuma *to get acquainted*, viivitama *to delay*, ühinema *to join*, hakkama saama *to cope*, hüvasti jätma *to say goodbye (to sb)*, juttu ajama *to talk*, peale hakkama *to do with sth*, rahul olema *to be satisfied*, toime tulema *to cope*, etc.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Neiu räägib **sõbrannaga**. | The girl is talking to her **girlfriend**. |
| Sõber tegeleb **spordiga**. | My friend does **sports**. |
| Tutvusin eile **huvitava kunstnikuga**. | I got acquainted with an interesting **artist** yesterday. |
| Ajasin **temaga** kaua juttu. | I talked to **him/her** for a long time. |
