## 1. Verbi põhivormid · Basic Verb Forms

The basic verb forms are the **-ma** and the **-da infinitive**, the **1st person present indicative**, and the **-tud participle**.

| Vorm | Sõna | English |
|---|---|---|
| -ma infinitive | hakkama | to begin |
| -da infinitive | hakata | to begin |
| 1st person present indicative | hakkan | I begin |
| -tud participle | hakatud | begun |

Usually dictionaries provide the first three of the basic forms of gradational verbs.

**The following forms are formed from the -ma infinitive:**

1. affirmative forms of the imperfect indicative — hakkama, hakata, hakkan, hakatud > hakka**sin** *to begin, to begin, I begin, begun > I began*
2. affirmative and negative forms of the present quotative — hakka/ma > hakka/**vat**, ei hakka/**vat** *to begin > they are said to begin*
3. the **-v** participle — hakka/ma > hakka/**v** *to begin > beginning*
4. **-ma** infinitive forms (**-mas, -mast, -maks, -mata**) — hakka/mas, hakka/mast, hakka/maks, hakka/mata *about to begin, from beginning, in order to begin, without beginning*

**The following forms are formed from the -da infinitive:**

1. the 2nd person plural imperative — haka/ta > (Te) haka/**ke**! Ärge hakake! *to begin > you (pl) begin! don't begin!*
2. the **-nud** participle — haka/ta > haka/**nud** *to begin > began*
3. the **-des** form — haka/ta > haka/**tes** *to begin > while beginning*

**The present indicative personal stem** is used to form:

1. the present indicative forms — hakka/n > ma hakka/**n**, sa hakka/**d**, ta hakka/**b**, me hakka/**me**, te hakka/**te**, nad hakka/**vad** *begin > I begin, you begin, he/she begins, we begin, you begin, they begin*
2. the 2nd person singular imperative — hakka/n > (Sa) hakka! Ära hakka! *I begin > you (sg) begin! don't begin!*
3. present conditional forms — hakka/n > hakka/ks(in), hakka/ks(id), hakka/ks, hakka/ks(ime), hakka/ks(ite), hakka/ks(id) *I begin > I would begin, you would begin, s/he would begin, we would begin, you would begin, they would begin*

**The -tud participle stem** is used to form all the **impersonal** forms.

| Vorm | Sõna | English |
|---|---|---|
| Present impersonal | haka/tud > haka/ta/kse, ei haka/ta | is begun, is not begun |
| Imperfect impersonal | haka/ti, ei haka/tud | was begun / was not begun |
| Perfect impersonal | on haka/tud, ei ole haka/tud | has been begun, has not been begun |
| Pluperfect impersonal | oli haka/tud, ei olnud haka/tud | had been begun, had not been begun |

**Verb Forms I** — *lugema "read"*

| -ma infinitive | -da infinitive |
|---|---|
| imperfect indicative: luge/**sin** *I read* | 2nd person plural imperative: luge/**ge**! *you read* |
| present quotative: luge/**vat** *is reported to read* | -nud participle: luge/**nud** *has read* |
| -v participle: luge/**v** *reading* | -des form: luge/**des** *reading* |
| forms of the -ma infinitive: luge/**mas**, luge/**mast**, luge/**maks**, luge/**mata** *is reading, from reading, in order to read, without reading* | |

**Verb Forms II**

| 1st person present indicative | -tud participle |
|---|---|
| present indicative: loe/**n**, loe/**d**, loe/**b**, loe/**me**, loe/**te**, loe/**vad** *I read, you read, s/he reads, we read, you read, they read* | present impersonal: loe/**takse**, ei loe/**ta** *is read, is not read* |
| 2nd person singular imperative: loe! *read!* | imperfect impersonal: loe/**ti**, ei loe/**tud** *was read, was not read* |
| present conditional: loe/**ks**(-in, -id, etc.) *I would read, you would read, etc.* | perfect impersonal: on loe/**tud**, ei ole loetud *has been read, has not been read* |
| | pluperfect impersonal: oli loe/**tud**, ei olnud loetud *had been read, had not been read* |

## 2. Indikatiivi preesens · Present Indicative

The present indicative is formed by adding the appropriate personal ending to the stem of the present indicative.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| elama, elada, ela/**n** | to live, to live, I live |

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ma **elan** Eestis. | I live in Estonia. |
| Sa **elad** Tartus. | You live in Tartu. |
| Ta **elab** linnas. | S/he lives in town. |
| Me **elame** Euroopas. | We live in Europe. |
| Te **elate** maal. | You live in the country. |
| Nad **elavad** uues majas. | They live in a new house. |

Negative forms have **no personal endings**.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ma (sa, ta, me, te, nad) **ei ela** Ameerikas. | I (you, he/she, we, you, they) don't live in America. |

| Pronoun | Personal ending | Pronoun | Personal ending |
|---|---|---|---|
| ma *I* | -n | me *we* | -me |
| sa *you* | -d | te *you* | -te |
| ta *he/she* | -b | nad *they* | -vad |

**Conjugation of the verb olema in the present:**

| Eesti keeles | English |
|---|---|
| ma olen | I am |
| sa oled | you are |
| ta on | he/she/it is |
| me oleme | we are |
| te olete | you are |
| nad on | they are |

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ma, sa, ta, me, te, nad **ei ole**. | I am not. You are not. He/she/it is not. We, you, they are not. |

## 3. Mitmesõnalised verbikonstruktsioonid · Multi-word Verb Constructions

Estonian multi-word verb constructions are the following.

**1. Phrasal verbs (ühendverb)** consisting of a verb and an adverb that usually constitute a semantic whole. The order of the words in phrasal verbs can vary in the sentence.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| alla kirjutama, alla kirjutada, ma kirjutan alla | to sign, I sign |

| Eesti keeles | English |
|---|---|
| ma kirjutan alla | I sign |
| sa kirjutad alla | you sign |
| ta kirjutab alla | he/she signs |
| me kirjutame alla | we sign |
| te kirjutate alla | you sign |
| nad kirjutavad alla | they sign |

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ma **kirjutan** avaldusele **alla**. | I'll **sign** the application. |
| Avaldusele peab **alla kirjutama**. | One must **sign** the application. |

More phrasal verbs: läbi arutama *discuss*, läbi lugema *read through*, läbi saama *get along*; sisse astuma *step inside*, sisse hingama *breathe in*, sisse magama *oversleep*; üle elama *survive/go through*, üle kuulama *interrogate*, üle minema *go over*; välja kannatama *endure*, välja mõtlema *come up with*, välja nägema *look*, etc.

**2. Idiomatic verb phrases (expression verbs) (väljendverb)** consisting of a verb and one or more nouns that form a semantic whole (usually metaphorical). Idiomatic verb phrases and phrasal verbs behave similarly in the sentence.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| meelde tulema | remember |
| silma torkama | stand out |
| pahaks panema | disapprove |

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Need sõnad **ei tule** meile **meelde**. | I can't **remember** these words. |
| Andekas näitleja **torkas** lavastajale **silma**. | The director **noticed** the talented actor. |
| Ma **ei pane** seda **pahaks**. | We don't **disapprove** it. |

## 4. Tulevik · Future

Estonian **lacks morphological forms for the future**. Usually the verb remains in the **present form**. Temporal adverbs indicating future are often used to express the future tense.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Me ei kasvata tomateid. | We don't grow tomatoes. |
| **Tuleval aastal** ei kasvata me tomateid. | **Next year** we won't grow tomatoes. |

Also the present forms of the verbs **jääma** *to remain*, **saama** *to become*, **minema** *to go*, **tulema** *to come* are used to express the future.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ta **jääb** haigeks. | He will fall ill. |
| Sa **saad** terveks. | You will get well. |

## 5. Infinitiiv e tegevusnimi · Infinitive

Estonian has two forms of the infinitive: the **-ma** and the **-da** infinitive.

**The -ma infinitive** is a basic verb form with the marker **-ma**. The -ma infinitive of gradational verbs is always in the **strong grade**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| rääkima | speak |
| hüppama | jump |

The -ma infinitive has **five declinable forms**: the -ma, -mas, -mast, -maks, and -mata forms.

**Forms of the -ma infinitive:**

| Vorm | Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|---|
| -ma infinitive | Lähen **sööma**. | I'm going and eat something. |
| -mas form | Ta on **söömas**. | He is (out) for lunch. |
| -mast form | Ta tuleb varsti **tantsimast**. | He will soon be back from dancing. |
| -maks form | Sõitsin Tartuse, **astumaks** ülikooli. | I travelled to Tartu in order to go to university. |
| -mata form | Ei saa elada **söömata**. | One can not live without eating. |

**-da Infinitive**

The -da infinitive is a basic verb form with the marker **-da, -ta,** or **-a**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| ela/da | to live |
| haka/ta | to begin |
| süü/a | to eat |

Usually the -da infinitive marker is **-da**: asuda *to be located*, rääkida *to talk*, pidada *to have to*, ütelda *to say*, jääda *to stay*, etc.

The **-ta** marker is used when the one-syllable verb stem ends in **-s**: pesta *to wash*, tõusta *to get up*. With gradational verbs **-ta** is added to the **weak stem**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| hüppama, **hüpata**, hüppan | to jump |
| hakkama, **hakata**, hakkan | to begin |

**-a marker** occurs in:

1. gradational verbs with one-syllable stems ending in **-t** or **-d**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| tundma, **tunda**, tunnen | to feel, to know |
| nutma, **nutta**, nutan | to cry |

2. verbs with stems with a long vowel or diphthong:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| viima, **viia**, viin | to take, to carry |
| sööma, **süüa**, söön | to eat |
| tooma, **tuua**, toon | to bring |

3. two-syllable verbs with sound changes in the stem:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| olema, **olla**, olen | to be |
| tulema, **tulla**, tulen | to come |
| minema, **minna**, lähen | to go |
| panema, **panna**, panen | to put, to place |
| surema, **surra**, suren | to die |

## 6. -des vorm · Gerund

The gerund **-des** is formed from the -da infinitive: **-ta > -tes, -da > -des, -a > -es**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| hüppama, hüpata, hüppan > hüpa**tes** | jumping |
| rääkima, rääkida, räägin > rääki**des** | talking |
| käima, käia, käin > käi**es** | walking |

The gerund expresses an action occurring **simultaneously** with the action of the main verb.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Nad läksid **naerdes** mööda teed. | They went **laughing** along the road. |
| **Oodates** hakkas tal külm. | He became cold while **waiting**. |

The use of the infinitive in the sentence is determined by the verb that is connected to the -ma or the -da infinitive. Usually, in simple sentences, a finite verb precedes a non-finite verb.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ma **armastan** palju **süüa** ja **magada**. | I **love to eat** a lot and **to sleep**. |
| Ma **sõidan** Pariisi **õppima**. | I'll go to Paris to study. |

## 7. Verbid, mis nõuavad -ma või -da infinitiivi · Verbs Combining with the -ma or the -da Infinitive

### -ma infinitiiv · -ma infinitive

**Verbs expressing motion:** sõitma *to drive, travel*, minema *to go*, tulema *to come*, kiirustama *to rush, to hurry*, ruttama *to rush, to hurry*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ta **sõidab** Itaaliasse **puhkama**. | S/he will travel to Italy for a vacation. |
| Me **läheme** restorani **sööma**. | We'll go to a restaurant to dine. |
| **Tule** minuga tähti **vaatama**. | Come with me to look up at the stars. |
| Nad **kiirustasid** sõbraga **kohtuma**. | They rushed to meet a friend. |
| Me **ruttame** koju süüa **tegema**. | We are rushing home to cook. |

**Verbs expressing obligation:** kohustatud olema *must, to be obliged*, pidama *must*, sundima *to force*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Sa oled **kohustatud** temaga **kohtuma**. | You are obliged to meet him/her. |
| Ta **peab** homme **esinema**. | He/She must perform tomorrow. |
| Keegi **ei sunni** neid **õppima**. | Nobody makes them study. |

**Verbs expressing the beginning of an action:** hakkama *to begin*, süttima *to catch fire*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| See neiu **hakkab** kergesti **naerma**. | This girl bursts out laughing easily. |
| Jaaniööl **süttisid** tuhanded lõkked **põlema**. | On Midsummer Night thousands of bonfires were lighted. |

**Other meanings:** ajama *to make (sb do sth), to drive*, õpetama *to teach*, jätma *to leave*, õppima *to learn*, jääma *to stay*, kutsuma *to invite*, saatma *to send*, panema *to make, put*, vaevuma *to bother*, viima *to take*, valmis olema *to be ready*, harjunud olema *to be used to*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Suits **ajas** teda **köhima**. | The smoke made him/her cough. |
| Kokk **õpetas** mind suppi **keetma**. | The cook taught me how to make soup. |
| **Jätsin** nad koju **magama**. | I left them home sleeping. |
| Lapsed **õpivad** joonistama. | The children are learning to draw. |
| Ema **jäi** koju kirju **kirjutama**. | Mother stayed home to write letters. |
| Dirigent **kutsub** kõiki kaasa **laulma**. | The conductor invites everybody to sing along. |
| Isa **saatis** lapsed **mängima**. | Dad sent the kids to play. |
| Kuu **paneb** järvevee **helkima**. | The moon makes the water of the lake shimmer. |
| Nad **ei vaevunud vastama**. | They didn't bother to answer. |
| Me **viisime** uued tuttavad **sööma**. | We took the new acquaintances out for dinner. |
| **Olge valmis põgenema**. | Be ready to flee. |
| Te **olete harjunud võitma**. | You are used to winning. |

### -da infinitiiv · -da infinitive

**Verbs expressing the wish (to do something):** soovima *to wish*, tahtma *to want, to wish*, soovitama *to advise*, paluma *to ask, to beg*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Mees **soovib** selle neiuga **tutvuda**. | The man wishes to get acquainted with this girl. |
| Ma **tahan** ülikooli **astuda**. | I want to go to university. |
| Meil **soovitati sõita** rongiga. | We were advised to take a train. |
| Võõrastel **paluti** majast **lahkuda**. | The strangers were asked to leave the house. |

**Verbs expressing feelings:** meeldima *to like*, armastama *to love*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Noortele **meeldib reisida**. | Young people like to travel. |
| Me **armastame** kinos **käia**. | We love going to the cinema. |

**Impersonal expressions:** on huvitav *is interesting*, on tore *is nice*, on kerge *is easy*, on raske *is hard*, on tähtis *is important*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| **On huvitav** uute inimestega **tutvuda**. | It is interesting to meet new people. |
| **On tore** pisut **puhata**. | It is nice to rest a little. |
| Teda **on kerge unustada**. | It's easy to forget him/her. |
| Sellest tööst **on raske loobuda**. | It is hard to give up this work. |
| **On tähtis** see võistlus **lõpetada**. | It is important to finish this competition. |

**Verbs expressing possibility:** võima *can, may*, tohtima *may, to be allowed*, suutma *to be able to, can*, saama *can, to be able to*, oskama *can, to be able to do sth*, jõudma *to manage to do sth*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Sa **võid** homme arstile **helistada**. | You can call the doctor tomorrow. |
| Kas ma **tohin** täna peole **minna**? | May I go to the party tonight? |
| Nad **ei suuda** praegu rohkem **süüa**. | They cannot eat more right now. |
| Siin **saab** ka käsi **pesta**. | You can wash your hands here as well. |
| Ta **ei osanud** küsimusele **vastata**. | S/he couldn't answer the question. |
| Õpilane **jõudis** raamatu läbi **lugeda**. | The student managed to finish the book. |

**Verbs expressing necessity:** on tarvis *need*, on vaja *need*, tuleb *must, to have to*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Meil **on tarvis** varem **lahkuda**. | We have to leave earlier. |
| Neil **on vaja** sind uuesti **näha**. | They need to see you again. |
| Töö **tuleb** uuesti läbi **lugeda**. | The paper needs to be read through again. |

**Verbs expressing intentions or plans:** püüdma *to try*, kavatsema *to plan*, proovima *to try*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| **Püüan** homme temaga **rääkida**. | I'll try to speak to him/her tomorrow. |
| Nad **kavatsevad** Tallinnasse **sõita**. | They plan to go to Tallinn. |
| **Proovige** ülikooli **astuda**. | Try to go to university. |
