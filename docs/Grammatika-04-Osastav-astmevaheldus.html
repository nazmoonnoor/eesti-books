How to publish

4 · Osastav. Noomeni astmevaheldus. Partitive Singular, Gradation of Nouns

Chapter 4 of the Eesti keele grammatika reference, book pages 13–17. How the partitive singular is formed and used, and the gradation (astmevaheldus) of nouns — quantity and quality alternation.

Estonian first, English alongside. Word triples are nominative : genitive : partitive (the genitive stem is often underlined in the book; here it is the middle form).

On this page: Osastava moodustamine · Osastava kasutamine · Astmevaheldus

1. Osastava moodustamine · Forming the partitive

The partitive singular answers the questions keda? whom?, mida? what?. It is formed by adding -t, -d or -da, or by means of the stem vowel.

Osastav -t

The partitive ending is -t in the following cases:

1) the word has three or more syllables in the genitive singular:

Sõna English
raamat : raamatu : raamatut book : of a book : book (part.)
maasikas : maasika : maasikat strawberry : of a strawberry : strawberry (part.)
hele : heleda : heledat bright : of bright : bright (part.)

2) a two-syllable word whose genitive first syllable has a long/overlong sound or a sound combination:

Sõna English
valge : valge : valget white : of white : white (part.)
roosa : roosa : roosat pink : of pink : pink (part.)

3) the word ends in -ne or -s in the nominative:

Sõna English
harjutus : harjutuse : harjutust exercise : of an exercise : exercise (part.)
naine : naise : naist woman : of a woman : woman (part.)
soodne : soodsa : soodsat favourable : of favourable : favourable (part.)

2. Osastava kasutamine · Using the partitive

The partitive is used:

  1. for the object when an action is not complete but progressive — Õpetaja kirjutab sel suvel õpikut. The teacher is writing a textbook this summer.
  2. when an action is directed towards a part of a thing (uncountable material) or a phenomenon — (partitive) Ema ostis lapsele jäätist. Mother bought her child some ice-cream. · cf. (genitive) Ema ostis lapsele jäätise. Mother bought her child an ice-cream.
  3. numeral + the partitive singular — Mul on üks tütar ja kaks poega. I have one daughter and two sons.
  4. amount + the partitive singular — Kui palju maksab kilo suhkrut? How much does a kilogramme of sugar cost? · Ma ostan pudeli mineraalvett. I'll buy a bottle of mineral water.
  5. negation + the partitive singular — Täna ei ole loengut. Today there is no lecture.

Verbs that require the partitive: armastama love, austama respect, eitama deny, hoidma hold, keep, huvitama interest, imetlema admire, kallistama hug, kartma fear, kiitma praise, kohtama meet, kuulama listen, laitma disapprove, mõjutama affect, mängima play, märkama notice, nautima enjoy, nägema see, ootama wait, otsima seek, pooldama favour, ravima treat, ründama attack, sallima tolerate, solvama offend, süüdistama accuse, taipama realize, taotlema apply for, teadma know, teenindama serve, toitma feed, tutvustama introduce, tähistama mark, celebrate, tänama thank, uurima study, vaatama look, vajama need, valdama possess, vihkama hate, vältima avoid, taga ajama chase, tähele panema notice, etc.

3. Noomeni astmevaheldus · Gradation of Nouns

Gradation does not occur in all word stems. Many words change only their inflectional endings and markers when declined or conjugated; a clear distinction between the first three cases is made only where there are stem changes. To tell whether a word is gradational, compare three forms — nominative, genitive, partitive. Estonian nouns alternate either from the strong grade → weak grade, or from the weak grade → strong grade.

  • Strong → weak (SG : WG : SG): nominative in the strong grade, genitive in the weak, partitive in the strong — TA (tugev aste) = kott, NA (nõrk aste) = koti, SG = kotti "bag".
  • Weak → strong (WG : SG : WG): nominative in the weak grade, genitive in the strong, partitive in the weak — mõte : mõtte : mõtet "thought".

The nominative and partitive always take the same grade.
