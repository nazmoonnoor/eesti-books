16 · Linnas (In town)

Chapter 16 of E nagu Eesti, book pages 132–137. Getting around town: reading city maps of Tallinn and Tartu, asking for and giving directions, buses, trams, taxis and parking, the kuhu? / kus? / kust? case endings all together, and the "must / can / may / want" verbs pidama · oskama · võima · saama · tohtima.

Estonian first. Open the 🇬🇧 Translation drop-downs for English, and the Answers drop-down only after trying an exercise.

2. Ma lähen

Street names take the same endings as other place words. With one-word names, both the adjective-like first part and the word tänav change.

Raatuse tänav Raatuse tänavale
Tehnika tänav Tehnika tänavale
Pikk tänav Pikale tänavale

3. Küsi teed

Dialoog 1 — Kuidas sõita bussijaama?

– Vabandage, kas te võiksite öelda, milline buss või tramm läheb bussijaama?
– Bussijaama saate trammiga number 2 või 4.
– Kas mõni buss ka sinna läheb?
– Jah, 17 ja 23 lähevad, aga bussipeatus on kaugemal Estonia ees. Siit on trammiga lihtsam minna. Ainult kolm peatust.
– Aitäh, ma siis lähen trammiga.

Dialoog 2 — Kuidas minna Raekoja platsile?

– Vabandage, kuidas ma saan Raekoja platsile?
– Minge üle ristmiku ja keerake ümber nurga vasakule. Siis minge otse edasi umbes 300 meetrit. Ja juba Raekoda paistabki.
– Selge. Nii et see ei ole väga kaugel?
– Ei, ehk ainult viie minuti tee.
– Tänan väga.

Dialoog 3 — Küsin teed

– Vabandage, kus asub tehnikaülikool?
– Oi, see on hoopis Mustamäel.
– Kuidas ma sinna saan?
– Kõige parem on minna trolliga number 3.
– Kas peatus on ülikooli ees?
– Ei, te peate maha minema Ehitajate tee peatuses. Sealt lähete üle tee ja natuke edasi läbi pargi.

Dialoog 4 — Otsime parkimiskohta

– Paistab, et oleme kohal.

🇬🇧 Translation

D1: – Excuse me, could you tell me which bus or tram goes to the bus station? / – You can get to the bus station by tram no. 2 or 4. / – Does any bus go there too? / – Yes, 17 and 23 go, but the bus stop is further away, in front of the Estonia. From here it's easier by tram. Only three stops. / – Thanks, I'll go by tram then.

D2: – Excuse me, how do I get to the Town Hall Square? / – Go across the junction and turn left round the corner. Then go straight ahead about 300 metres. And the Town Hall already comes into view. / – I see. So it's not very far? / – No, perhaps only five minutes' walk. / – Thank you very much.

D3: – Excuse me, where is the technical university? / – Oh, that's actually in Mustamäe. / – How do I get there? / – Best is to take trolleybus no. 3. / – Is the stop in front of the university? / – No, you have to get off at the Ehitajate tee stop. From there you go across the road and a bit further through the park. / – How long does the trolley take roughly? / – Not more than 20 minutes.

D4: – Looks like we've arrived. / – Yes, this is house no. 21. But where can we park? / – There's a no-parking sign here, you can't park in front of the house. / – Maybe you can there, by the side of the street? / – Yes, but all the spaces there are full. / – Look, a bit further on there's a paid car park! Let's drive there. / – OK, let's hope there are free spaces there.

5. Tasuma · keerama · tohtima

Three useful synonyms:

  • tasuma = maksma (to pay) — Kas te tasute sularahas või maksate kaardiga?
  • keerama = pöörama (to turn) — Keera vasakule või pööra paremale, koju jõuad lõpuks ikka.
  • tohtima = võima (to be allowed) — Miks vanemad tohivad kaua väljas olla, aga lapsed ei või?

🇬🇧 Translation of the jokes

Student and taxi: A student gets into a taxi: "Narva Road 25, please." The driver: "But this is already Narva Road 25." The student: "Very good. Just don't drive so terribly fast next time."

Left turn: A policeman stops a car. The driver rolls down the window and asks: "Did I do something wrong?" "You did." "Isn't there a left turn here?" "There is, but it's a paid one."

Car and truck: Wife to husband: "You can't imagine what happened! A big truck ran into our car. There's only bits left of our car." "Never mind, dear, the main thing is that you're alive and well. What happened to the other driver?" "There was nobody in it. That car was parked in front of the shop."

🇬🇧 Translation

D5: – Excuse me, how do I get to the Raatuse dormitory? / – It's easy, just across the river. First go to the Town Hall Square and by the yellow house turn round the corner. / – Ah, and then across the bridge, right? / – Exactly, go straight across the arch bridge and from there on across the street. / – Is it on the left or the right side? / – On the left. The building has a big sign on it too. You'll see it right away.

Rhyme: If you want to cross the road, then pay good attention. Look left first, then turn your head right! And then go straight across, if no fast vehicle is coming.
