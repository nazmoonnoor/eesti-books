# 5 · Kohakäänded. Locative Cases

Chapter 5 of the *Eesti keele grammatika* reference, **book pages 18–22**. The six locative cases: the internal set (illative, inessive, elative) and the external set (allative, adessive, ablative) — how each is formed and used, with summary tables.

> Estonian first, English alongside. Word forms are **nominative : genitive : partitive > case form**.

Estonian has two sets of locative cases. The **internal** locative cases (*sisekohakäänded*) are the **illative, inessive, elative**. The **external** locative cases (*väliskohakäänded*) are the **allative, adessive, ablative**.

## 1. Sisseütlev · Illative

**kellesse?** *into whom?*, **millesse?** *into what?*, **kuhu?** *where to?*

**1)** The illative is formed from the **genitive stem** by adding the ending **-sse**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| teater : teatri : teatrit > teatri**sse** | into the theatre |
| hammas : hamba : hammast > hamba**sse** | into the tooth |

**2)** The **short form** of the illative has no ending. It can be formed if the word has two syllables in the genitive, and from some one-syllable words. Where gradational words have the genitive in the weak grade and the partitive in the strong grade, the short illative often **coincides with the partitive**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| kott : koti : kotti > kotti | into the bag |
| mets : metsa : metsa > metsa | into the forest |
| linn : linna : linna > linna | to town |
| aed : aia : aeda > aeda | into the garden |
| kool : kooli : kooli > kooli | to school |
| jaam : jaama : jaama > jaama | to the station |
| kapp : kapi : kappi > kappi | into the cabinet |

The short illative of some two-syllable words is formed by **doubling the stem consonant**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| maja : maja : maja > ma**jj**a | into the house |
| rida : rea : rida > ri**tt**a | into the row |
| tuba : toa : tuba > tu**pp**a | into the room |
| käsi : käe : kätt > kä**tt**e | into a hand |
| tuli : tule : tuld > tu**ll**e | into the fire |
| kino : kino : kino > kin**n**o | to the cinema |

The short illative in **-de** occurs in the words **uus** *new*, **keel** *language*, **suur** *large* (and similar):

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| uus : uue : uut > uu**de** | into new |
| keel : keele : keelt > kee**lde** | into a language |
| suur : suure : suurt > suu**rde** | into big/large |

The short illative of some one-syllable words with a long vowel ends in **-hu, -ha, -he**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| suu : suu : suud > su**hu** | into a mouth |
| maa : maa : maad > ma**ha** | onto the ground |
| pea : pea : pead > pä**he** | into the head; by heart |

**The illative** denotes **movement into something** — *Sõitsime **linna**.* We drove **into the city**.

Verbs that require the illative: armuma *fall in love*, haigestuma *fall ill*, investeerima *invest*, kiinduma *become fond of*, nakatuma *get infected*, puutuma *touch, concern*, sekkuma *interfere*, suhtuma *have an attitude*, surema *die*, uskuma *believe*, etc.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ma suhtun **temasse** hästi. | I think well of **him/her**. |
| Nad nakatusid **grippi**. | They caught **the flu**. |
| Inimesed kiinduvad oma **lemmikloomadesse**. | People become fond of **their pets**. |

## 2. Seesütlev · Inessive

**kelles?** *in(side) whom?*, **milles?** *in(side) what?*, **kus?** *where?*

The inessive is formed from the **genitive stem** and ends in **-s**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| teater : teatri : teatrit > teatri**s** | at the theatre |
| mets : metsa : metsa > metsa**s** | in the forest |

**The inessive** denotes **location inside something** — *Sõber on täna **kinos**.* My friend is **at the cinema** today.

Verbs requiring the inessive: käima *go*, veenduma *be convinced*, pettuma *be disappointed*, etc.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Lapsed käivad **koolis**. | Children go **to school**. |
| Õpetaja pettus **õpilases**. | The teacher was disappointed **in the student**. |

## 3. Seestütlev · Elative

**kellest?** *(out) of whom?*, **millest?** *(out) of what?*, **kust?** *where from?*

The elative is formed from the **genitive stem** by adding the ending **-st**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| pea : pea : pead > pea**st** | from the head; from memory |
| rida : rea : rida > rea**st** | from the row |
| saabas : saapa : saabast > saapa**st** | from the boot |

**The elative is used:**

1. to denote **movement out of something** — *Ema tuleb **teatrist**.* Mother is coming **from the theatre**.
2. to denote **material** — *See vaas on **klaasist**.* This vase is **(made) of glass**.
3. to indicate the **object talked or thought about** — *Jutusta mulle sellest **filmist**.* Tell me **about this movie**.
4. to denote the **beginning of a period of time** — *Olen puhkusel 1. (esimesest) **juunist**.* I'm on holiday **from June 1**.

Verbs that take the elative: keelduma *refuse*, koosnema *consist of*, leidma *find*, loobuma *give up*, mööduma *pass*, olenema *depend*, sõltuma *depend*, põlvnema *descend (from)*, saama *become; receive*, toibuma *recover*, toituma *feed (on sth)*, tüdinema *get bored (with)*, unistama *dream*, vaikima *be silent*, värisema *tremble*, väsima *get tired*, üllatuma *be surprised*, lahku minema *split up; divorce*, lugu pidama *respect*, maha jääma *fall behind*, osa võtma *take part*, puudust tundma *miss (sth/sb)*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| **Sinust** saab hea õpetaja. | **You** will be a good teacher. |
| **Meist** ei sõltu midagi. | Nothing depends **on us**. |
| Firma keeldus **koostööst** ülikooliga. | The company refused **to cooperate** with the university. |
| Tüdruk väriseb **külmast**. | The girl is shivering **with cold**. |
| Õpilane tunneb puudust **headest õpikutest**. | The student misses **good textbooks**. |

## 4. Alaleütlev · Allative

**kellele?** *(to) whom?*, **millele?** *on(to) what?*, **kuhu?** *to where?*

The allative is formed from the **genitive stem** by adding the ending **-le**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| vend : venna : venda > venna**le** | to the brother |
| tütar : tütre : tütart > tütre**le** | to the daughter |

**The allative is used:**

1. to denote **movement on(to) a surface** — *Poisid lähevad **staadionile**.* The boys are going **to the stadium**.
2. to indicate the **person or object to whom / to which something is given** — *Isa andis **pojale** raha.* Father gave some money **to his son**. · ***Koolile** pandi uus nimi.* A new name was given **to the school**.

Verbs and adjectives that take the allative: järgnema *follow*, lootma *hope*, mõjuma *influence*, meenuma *recall*, naeratama *smile*, viitama *refer*, alla kirjutama *sign*, etc.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| **Ööle** järgneb päev. | **Night** is followed by day. |

## 5. Alalütlev · Adessive

**kellel?** *by whom?*, **millel?** *on what?*, **kus?** *where?*

The adessive is formed from the **genitive stem** by adding the ending **-l**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| meri : mere : merd > mere**l** | at sea |
| mägi : mäe : mäge > mäe**l** | on the hill |
| arst : arsti : arsti > arsti**l** | at the doctor's |

**The adessive is used:**

1. to denote **location on a surface** — *Poisid on **staadionil**.* The boys are **at the stadium**.
2. to denote **belonging or possession** — *Minu **õel** on mitu head sõpra.* My **sister** has several good friends.
3. to express **time** — *Me läheme tööle **hommikul** ja tuleme tagasi **õhtul**.* We go to work **in the morning** and come back **in the evening**.

Verbs that require the adessive: käima *to go*, põhinema *to be based*, etc.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Isa käib **tööl**. | Father goes **to work**. |
| Minu arvamus temast põhineb sinu **sõnadel**. | My opinion of him/her is based **on your words**. |

## 6. Alaltütlev · Ablative

**kellelt?** *off / away from whom?*, **millelt?** *off / away from what?*, **kust?** *from where?*

The ablative is formed from the **genitive stem** by adding the ending **-lt**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| laud : laua : lauda > laua**lt** | from the table |
| tänav : tänava : tänavat > tänava**lt** | from the street |
| liige : liikme : liiget > liikme**lt** | from a member |

**The ablative is used:**

1. to denote **movement off a surface or away from a situation** — *Ta tuli **maalt** linna.* He came to town **from the country**. · *Õhtul tulid poisid **staadionilt** koju.* In the evening the boys came home **from the stadium**.
2. to indicate the **person from whom the action originates** — *Ta sai **õelt** kirja.* He received a letter **from his sister**.

Verbs that take the ablative: pärima *to inherit*, etc.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ta päris **sugulastelt** maja. | He inherited the house **from his relatives**. |

## 7. Kohakäänete kokkuvõte · Summary

**Sisekohakäänded · Internal locative cases** — *nominative: kool "school", genitive: kooli*

| Kääne | Lõpp | Küsimus | Vorm | Näide |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Illative | -sse, ø | to where? | kooli ~ kooli/sse | Hommikul lähevad lapsed **kooli**. *In the morning the kids go to school.* |
| Inessive | -s | where? | kooli/s | Lapsed on **koolis**. *The kids are in school.* |
| Elative | -st | from where? | kooli/st | Õhtul tulevad lapsed **koolist**. *In the evening the kids come from school.* |

**Väliskohakäänded I · External locative cases I** — *nominative: laud "table", genitive: laua*

| Kääne | Lõpp | Küsimus | Vorm | Näide |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Allative | -le | on(to) what? | laua/le | Pane tassid **lauale**. *Put the cups on the table.* |
| Adessive | -l | on what? | laua/l | Tassid on juba **laual**. *The cups are already on the table.* |
| Ablative | -lt | off what? | laua/lt | Koristasime tassid **laualt**. *We cleared the cups away from the table.* |

**Väliskohakäänded II · External locative cases II** — *nominative: sõber "friend", genitive: sõbra*

| Kääne | Lõpp | Küsimus | Vorm | Näide |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Allative | -le | to whom? | sõbra/le | Andsin **sõbrale** raamatu. *I gave my friend a book.* |
| Adessive | -l | by whom? | sõbra/l | **Sõbral** on uus auto. *My friend has a new car.* |
| Ablative | -lt | from whom? | sõbra/lt | Laenasin **sõbralt** raha. *I borrowed some money from my friend.* |
