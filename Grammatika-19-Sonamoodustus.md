Chapter 19 of the *Eesti keele grammatika* reference, **book pages 71–79** — the final chapter of the grammar book. How Estonian derives nouns, adjectives, adverbs, and verbs from other words via suffixes, the one meaning-modifying prefix/suffix pair, and how compound words are built.

> Estonian first, English alongside. Word forms are given as **base word > derivative**, with declension triples (nominative : genitive : partitive) where the base is a noun.

## 1. Substantiivisufiksid e nimisõnaliited · Nominal Suffixes

### 1.1 Derivatives Denoting Action

The suffixes of this group derive **nouns from verbs**.

**-mine** — used to derive nouns from **all verbs**; expresses actions in a general sense.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| laulma > laulmine | to sing > singing |
| õmblema > õmblemine | to sew > sewing |

**-us** — generally added to consonant stems; the derivational base is usually the verb. Derivatives with -us denote:

a) the process of an action:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| õpetama > õpetus | to teach > teaching |

They often occur as the first component of compounds: kirjutama > kirjutuslaud *to write > writing desk*; puhastama > puhastusvahend *to clean > cleaning agent*.

b) an instance of an action:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| algama > algus | to begin > beginning |
| suudlema > suudlus | to kiss > a kiss |

c) the result of an action:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| joonistama > joonistus | to draw > a drawing |
| luuletama > luuletus | to compose poetry > poem |

d) the state resulting from an action:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| väsima > väsimus | get tired > tiredness |

The derivational base of -us can also be an **adjectival stem**, denoting qualities:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| suur > suurus | large > size |
| tark > tarkus | wise > wisdom |

**-is** — denotes mainly the **result of an action**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| tuletama > tuletis | to derive > derivative |
| jäätama > jäätis | to freeze > ice cream |

### 1.2 Derivatives Denoting Persons

**-ja** — can be added to the -ma infinitive stem of **all verbs**; denotes the agent of an action in a general sense.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| mängima > mängija | to play > player |
| õpetama > õpetaja | to teach > teacher |

**-lane (-lanna)** — denotes **nationality or origin**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| Eesti > eestlane, eestlanna | Estonia > Estonian |
| Tallinn > tallinlane, tallinlanna | Tallinn > Tallinner |

**-line** — usually added to noun stems; most such derivatives denote the agent of a **temporary action**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| pidu, peo, pidu > peoline | party > party-goer |
| seen, seene, seent > seeneline | mushroom > mushroom-picker |

**-ur** — added to both verbs and declinable words; denotes the **occupation or activity** of a person.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| õppima > õppur | to study > student |
| jalgratas > jalgrattur | bicycle > cyclist |

**-nik** — usually added to nouns; most derivatives denote the **profession or field of activity** of a person.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| kunst > kunstnik | art > artist |
| aed > aednik | garden > gardener |

### 1.3 Derivatives Denoting Place and Group

**-la** — added to both declinable words and verbs; denotes a specific **place, building, or room**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| haige > haigla | patient > hospital |
| ujuma > ujula | to swim > swimming pool |

Estonia has many place names with the suffix -la: Mustla, Rapla, Antsla.

**-kond** — generally added to nouns; most derivatives denote **groups of people**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| mees > meeskond | man > team |
| pere > perekond | family |
| paat > paatkond | boat > boat crew |

**-stik** — added to noun bases; mostly denotes **groups of items**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| sõna, sõna, sõna > sõnastik | word > dictionary |
| täht, tähe, tähte > tähestik | letter > alphabet |
| mägi, mäe, mäge > mäestik | mountain > mountain range |

**-ik** — belongs to different semantic groups:

a) groups of trees or forests:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| mänd, männi, mändi > männik | pine > pine forest |
| kuusk, kuuse, kuuske > kuusik | spruce > spruce forest |

b) places:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| kohv, kohvi, kohvi > kohvik | coffee > café |
| rand, ranna, randa > rannik | shore; beach > coast |

c) things:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| õppima > õpik | to study > textbook |
| tuul > tuulik | wind > windmill |

d) persons:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| saatma > saadik | to send > deputy; envoy |
| jooma > joodik | to drink > heavy drinker, alcoholic |

## 2. Adjektiivisufiksid e omadussõnaliited · Adjectival Suffixes

**-ne** — added to noun bases; commonly denotes:

a) contact with a substance or inclusion of a substance:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| veri, vere, verd > verine | blood > bloody |
| kuld, kulla, kulda > kuldne | gold > golden |
| sool, soola, soola > soolane | salt > salty |

b) measurement:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| aasta, aasta, aastat > aastane | year > year-old |
| kaks päeva, kahe päeva, kahte päeva > kahepäevane | two days > two-day |
| kolm kilo, kolme kilo, kolme kilo > kolmekilone | three kilos > three-kilo |

**-line** — usually added to noun bases; the meaning depends on the base word:

a) presence of a physical feature:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| lill, lille, lille > lilleline | flower > floral, flowery |
| ruut, ruudu, ruutu > ruuduline | square > chequered; squared |
| suur nina, suure nina, suurt nina > suureninaline | big nose > big-nosed |

b) connection to a field or area:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| ajalugu, ajaloo, ajalugu > ajalooline | history > historical; historic |
| keemia, keemia, keemiat > keemiline | chemistry > chemical |

**-lik**

1) the derivational base is a **noun**:

a) similarity to the base word, referring to a similar character, behaviour, and nature:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| naine, naise, naist > naiselik | woman > feminine |
| laps, lapse, last > lapselik | child > childlike |

b) being characteristic to a field or area, or belonging to somebody or something:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| pidu, peo, pidu > pidulik | party > festive |
| riik, riigi, riiki > riiklik | state > national |

2) the derivational base is a **verb** — the action denotes something characteristic of somebody or something:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| lõpetama > lõplik | to finish > final |
| mõtlema > mõtlik | to think > thoughtful |
| kartma > kartlik | to fear > fearful |

**-tu** — added to nouns and verbs; these adjectival derivatives denote **lack** of what the base word denotes.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| sisu, sisu, sisu > sisutu | meaning > meaningless |
| õnn, õnne, õnne > õnnetu | happiness > unhappy |
| kord, korra, korda > korratu | order > disorderly |
| ravima > ravimatu | to cure > incurable |
| tundma > tundmatu | to know > unknown |

**-kas**

1) noun + -kas — a bigger quantity or abundance of the quality expressed by the base word:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| puus, puusa, puusa > puusakas | hip > broad-hipped |
| seks, seksi, seksi > seksikas | sex > sexy |
| edu, edu, edu > edukas | success > successful |
| mahl, mahla, mahla > mahlakas | juice > juicy |

2) adjective + -kas — a **lesser than usual** quality:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| punane > punakas | red > reddish |
| hapu > hapukas | sour > sourish |

**Prefix eba-** — Estonian has one prefix, **eba-**. It is added:

1) to an adjective denoting **oppositeness**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| aus > ebaaus | honest > dishonest |
| meeldiv > ebameeldiv | agreeable > disagreeable |
| kindel > ebakindel | certain > uncertain |

2) to some nouns:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| õnn > ebaõnn | fortune > misfortune |
| usk > ebausk | faith > superstition |

## 3. Adverbisufiksid e määrsõnaliited · Adverbial Suffixes

The most common adverbial suffixes are **-sti** and **-lt**. These derivatives denote manner.

**-sti** — added to an adjective base.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| halb, halva, halba > halvasti | bad > badly, poorly |

**-lt** — added to an adjective base.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| vaikne, vaikse, vaikset > vaikselt | quiet > quietly |

## 4. Verbisufiksid e tegusõnaliited · Verb Suffixes

**-ta- (-da-, -sta-, -nda-)**

1) verb + -ta-: **intransitive verb + -ta- > transitive verb**

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| ärkama > äratama | to wake up > to wake sb up |
| kasvama > kasvatama | to grow > to grow sb/sth |
| lõppema > lõpetama | to end > to finish |

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Laps **ärkab**. | The child wakes up. |
| Ema **äratab** lapse. | The mother wakes up the child. |

2) noun + -ta-: the base word denotes an **instrument**, and the derivative denotes an action related to the instrument:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| suusk, suusa, suuska > suusatama | ski > to ski |
| kreem, kreemi, kreemi > kreemitama | cream > to cream |

3) adjective or noun + -ta-: derivatives denoting **being in a state or situation**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| kaval > kavaldama | cunning > to outwit |
| suvi > suvitama | summer > to summer, to spend the summer |

**-u-**

1) verb + -u-: **reflexive verbs**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| muutma > muutuma | to change sth > to change |
| hoidma > hoiduma | to keep; to hold > to avoid |
| peatama > peatuma | to stop sth > to stop |

2) noun + -u-: denotes a **change of state**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| külm > külmuma | cold > to freeze |
| vaene > vaesuma | poor > to impoverish |
| rand > randuma | shore > to land |

3) onomatopoeic base + -u-: denotes **recurrent actions**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| mürts > mürtsuma | a thump > to thump |
| krooks > krooksuma | a croak > to croak |

**-le-** — the derivational base is a verb or a declinable stem; these verbs denote **recurrent actions**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| lendama > lendlema | to fly > to flutter, to fly about |
| laisk > laisklema | lazy > to idle |

**-ise-** — onomatopoeic base + -ise-; denotes **continuous actions**.

mühisema *to roar*, virisema *to grouch*, värisema *to tremble*

**-ata-** — the derivational base is a verb or onomatopoeic stem; denotes a **momentary action**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| hüüdma > hüüatama | to shout > to give a shout |
| naerma > naeratama | to laugh > to smile |
| nuuksuma > nuuksatama | to sob > to give a sob |

**-ne-** — added to the base of declinable words, verbs, or adverbs; such verbs denote a **change of state** in the subject.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| noor > noorenema | young > to become younger |
| odav > odavnema | cheap > to become cheaper |
| rahu > rahunema | calm, peace > to calm down |

**-tse-** — added to a verb or a declinable word; denotes **continuous gradual actions**.

ravitsema *to heal*, tülitsema *to quarrel*, lõbutsema *to have fun*.

Many verbs with -tse- have a **derogatory connotation**: sigatsema *to act like a pig*, ülbitsema *to be arrogant*, tigetsema *to be mean*, äritsema *to deal in sth*.

## 5. Tähendust modifitseeriv sufiks · Suffixes that Modify the Meaning

**-ke(ne)** — used to turn a noun into the **diminutive**. The part of speech of the derivative does not change.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| ema > emakene | mother > mommy |
| sõber > sõbrakene | friend > buddy |
| ilus > ilusakene | pretty > pretty (diminutive) |
| rõõmus > rõõmsakene | happy > happy (diminutive) |

## 6. Liitsõna · Compound Words

Standard Estonian has many derivatives and compounds. A compound usually consists of **two or sometimes more** simple words written as **one word**.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| kesk/linn | city centre |
| laste/aia/laps | kindergarten child |
| maa/ilma/meistri/võistlused | world championships |

Usually the **first component** of a compound (attributive word) extends the **second component** (head).

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| naiste/rätsep | women's tailor |
| meeste/rätsep | men's tailor |

The first component of a compound is usually in the **nominative**, the **genitive singular**, or the **genitive plural**. The first component generally remains unchanged.

1) The first part is in the **nominative singular**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| mees + õpetaja > meesõpetaja | male teacher |
| samet + kleit > sametkleit | velvet dress |
| magus + toit > magustoit | dessert |
| kallis + kivi > kalliskivi | jewel, precious stone |
| sidrun + kollane > sidrunkollane | lemon yellow |
| kuri + kaval > kurikaval | sly |

2) The first component is in the **genitive singular**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| looma + aed > loomaaed | zoo |
| risti + usk > ristiusk | Christianity |
| kassi + kuld > kassikuld | tinsel |
| alkoholi + vaba > alkoholivaba | alcohol-free, non-alcoholic |
| kulla + kamber > kullakamber | gold vault |
| suure + jooneline > suurejooneline | spectacular |

3) The first component is in the **genitive plural**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| laste + arst > lastearst | paediatrician |
| naiste + arst > naistearst | gynaecologist |
| võõraste + maja > võõrastemaja | guesthouse |
| noorte + saade > noortesaade | youth show |

Sometimes the base of the first component has **lost some material**:

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| hiline + sügis > hilissügis | late autumn |
| sinine + sukk > sinisukk | bluestocking |
| võõras + sõna > võõrsõna | foreign word |
