## 1. Adverb e määrsõna · Adverb

### Lokaaladverb e kohamäärsõna · Locative Adverbs

Locative adverbs denote spatial relations. They are declined in **three locative cases**, and answer the questions **kuhu?** *where to?*, **kus?** *where?*, or **kust?** *where from?*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Me ei saa **kaugele** minna. | We cannot go **far**. |
| See päev on veel **kaugel**. | This day is still **far away**. |
| **Kaugelt** paistab kõrge mägi. | A high mountain can be seen **at a distance**. |

| Kuhu? Where to? | Kus? Where? | Kust? Where from? |
|---|---|---|
| siia *(to) here* | siin *here* | siit *from here* |
| sinna *(to) there* | seal *there* | sealt *from there* |
| lähedale *(to) near* | lähedal *near* | lähedalt *from near* |
| lähemale *(to) nearer* | lähemal *nearer* | lähemalt *from nearer* |
| kaugele *(to) far* | kaugel *far* | kaugelt *from far* |
| üles *(to) up, above* | üleval *up, above* | ülevalt *from above* |
| alla *(to) down* | all *down, under* | alt *from under* |
| ette *(to) ahead* | ees *ahead, in front* | eest *from ahead* |

The three cases are **not distinguished** in: tagasi *back*, edasi *forth*.

### Temporaaladverb e ajamäärsõna · Temporal Adverbs

Temporal adverbs answer the questions **millal?** *when?*, **kui kaua?** *(for) how long?*, **kui sageli?** *how often?*, **mis ajaks?** *for what time?*.

homme *tomorrow*, täna *today*, eile *yesterday*, ammu *long ago*, sageli *often*, tihti *often*, harva *seldom*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Me pole **kaua** kohtunud. | We haven't met **for a long time**. |
| **Varsti** sõidame maale. | **Soon** we will drive to the country. |

### Viisi- ja seisundimäärsõnad · Adverbs of Manner and Condition

Adverbs of manner and condition answer the questions **kuidas?** *how?*, **mil viisil?** *in what way?*, **millises olekus?** *in what condition?*.

hästi *well*, halvasti *badly*, ilusti *nicely*, salaja *secretly*, vaikselt *quietly*, segamini *in a mess*, lõbusalt *happily*, kurvalt *sadly*, paljapäi *bareheaded*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ta lamas **selili**. | He/she was lying **on his/her back**. |
| Laps naeris **valjusti**. | The child was laughing **aloud**. |
| Ta on **ärkvel**. | He/she is **awake**. |

### Hulga- ja määramäärsõnad · Adverbs of Quantity and Degree

Adverbs of quantity and degree answer the questions **kui palju?** *how much?*, **missugusel hulgal?** *to what extent?*.

kolmekesi *three of us*, üksinda *alone*, natuke *a little*, palju *a lot*, väga *very*, võrdlemisi *rather*, üsna *rather*, märksa *somewhat*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Lapse riided olid **täiesti** määrdunud. | The child's clothes were **completely** dirty. |
| Nad eelistavad olla **kahekesi**. | They prefer to be alone **(two of them)**. |

## 2. Pre- ja postpositsioonid e ees- ja tagasõnad · Prepositions and Postpositions

Prepositions and postpositions belong to the noun in the sentence. **Estonian has more postpositions than prepositions.**

### 1) Ruumisuhted · Expressing Spatial Relations

Postpositions expressing spatial relations have **three forms**.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Maja **ette** sõitis auto. | A car rolled **to the front of** the house. |
| Ta istutas puud maja **taha**. | He planted the trees **behind** the house. |
| Istun oma sõbra **kõrval**. | I'm sitting **next to** my friend. |
| Maja **taga** on mets. | There is a forest **behind** the house. |
| Võtsin raamatu laua **pealt**. | I took the book **from** the table. |
| Tulin maja **tagant**. | I came **from behind** the house. |

**Postpositions requiring the genitive:**

| Kuhu? Where to? | Kus? Where? | Kust? Where from? |
|---|---|---|
| sisse *into* | sees *in, inside* | seest *from inside* |
| alla *(to) down, under, below* | all *down, under, below* | alt *from below* |
| kohale *(to) over; into place* | kohal *over; in place* | kohalt *from over; from place* |
| ette *to the front of, ahead* | ees *in front of, ahead* | eest *from ahead* |
| taha *(to) back* | taga *back* | tagant *from the back* |
| järele *to after* | järel *after* | järelt *from after* |
| peale *onto* | peal *on* | pealt *off* |
| keskele *to the middle of* | keskel *in the middle of* | keskelt *from the middle of* |
| hulka *(to) among* | hulgas *among* | hulgast *from among* |
| sekka *(to) among* | seas *among* | seast *from among* |
| kõrvale *beside* | kõrval *beside* | kõrvalt *from beside* |
| juurde *to* | juures *at* | juurest *from* |
| poole *towards* | pool *by* | poolt *from* |
| külge *to* | küljes *attached to* | küljest *off* |
| kätte *into the hands of* | käes *in the hands of* | käest *from the hands of* |
| lähedale *(to) near* | lähedal *near* | lähedalt *from near* |
| ligidale *(to) near* | ligidal *near* | ligidalt *from near* |
| vahele *to between* | vahel *between* | vahelt *from between* |
| äärde *to* | ääres *at* | äärest *from* |
| vastu *to against* | vastas *against* | vastast *from against* |
| ümber *around* | ümber *around* | ümbert *from around* |

**Prepositions requiring the genitive:** läbi *through*, üle *over*, ümber *around*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Sõidame **läbi** küla, **ümber** kiriku ja siis **üle** mäe. | We'll drive **through** the village, **around** the church, and then **over** the hill. |

**Prepositions that require the partitive:** keset ~ kesk *in the middle of*, mööda *along*, piki *along*, vastu *against*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ta jäi **keset** tuba seisma. | He remained standing **in the middle of** the room. |
| Ta jalutas **mööda** tänavat. | He was walking **along** the street. |

**Prepositions requiring the terminative:** kuni *as far as, until*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Minge **kuni** selle majani, sealt pöörake vasakule. | Go **as far as** that house, then turn left. |

### 2) Ajasuhted · Expressing Temporal Relations

**Postpositions requiring the genitive:** eest *ago*, jooksul ~ kestel *during*, pärast *after*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Kohtusime aasta **eest**. | We met a year **ago**. |
| Kohtume aasta **pärast**. | We'll meet next year. |

**Postpositions that require the elative:** saadik ~ peale *since*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Sellest päevast **peale** on ta töötu. | He has been unemployed **since** that day. |

**Prepositions that take the partitive:** enne *before*, pärast ~ peale *after*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| **Enne** eksamit ta närveerib. | He is nervous **before** the exam. |
| **Pärast** tööd lähme sööma. | We'll go and dine **after** work. |

### 3) Põhjuse ja eesmärgi suhted · Expressing Causal Relations and Purpose

**Postpositions requiring the genitive:** pärast *because of*, tõttu *because of*, jaoks ~ tarvis *for*, heaks *for the benefit of*, puhul *in case of*, põhjal *on the basis of*, üle *over*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ta muretseb oma poja **pärast**. | He/she is worried **about** his/her son. |
| Ta leidis oma poja **jaoks** töökoha. | He/she found a job **for** his/her son. |

### 4) Hulgasuhted · Expressing Quantity Relations

**Postpositions requiring the genitive:** kaupa ~ viisi *in / by*, ümber *around*, võrra *by*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Inimesi haigestus sadade **kaupa/viisi**. | People fell ill **by hundreds**. |

**Prepositions that take the genitive:** alla *down*, üle *over*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Päeval on **üle** kahekümne kraadi sooja. | During the day it is **twenty degrees above** zero. |

### 5) Muud suhted · Other Relations

**Postpositions requiring the genitive:** kohta *about*, suhtes *concerning*, poolest *as for*, järgi *according to*, kaudu *by, via*, asemel *instead of*, eest *for*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Kuulsime uute naabrite **kohta** palju häid sõnu. | We heard many good words **about** our new neighbours. |
| Tema **asemel** sõidaksin Tartu **kaudu**. | If I were him, I would take the route **via** Tartu. |
| Meie **poolest** võib ta teha, mida tahab. | **As far as** we are concerned, he/she may do what he/she wants. |
| Keegi peab selle **eest** vastutama. | Somebody has to take responsibility **for** this. |
| Neil pole selle inimese **suhtes** oma arvamust. | They do not have an opinion **about** this person. |

**Prepositions requiring the genitive:** peale *apart from*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| **Peale** autojuhi ei tundnud keegi teed. | **Apart from** the driver nobody knew the way. |

**Prepositions taking the comitative:** koos ~ ühes *together, with*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Kas õunu süüakse **koos/ühes** seemnetega? | Are apples eaten **together** with the seeds? |

**Prepositions requiring the abessive:** ilma *without*.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Ma ei saa läbi **ilma** sinu abita. | I can't manage **without** your help. |

## 3. Konjunktsioon e sidesõna · Conjunctions

Estonian has about twenty conjunctions: ja ~ ning *and*, ega *nor/or*, ehk *maybe*, või *or*, kui *than*, et *that*, aga *but*, vaid *but*, kuid *however*, ent *yet*, kuna *because*, kuigi ~ ehkki *although, though*, nagu *as*, etc.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Mina läksin tööle, **aga** tütar kooli. | I went to work, **but** my daughter went to school. |
| Ta ei olnud eile õhtul teatris, **vaid** kinos *(negative sentence)*. | Yesterday evening he wasn't at the theatre **but** at the movies. |
| Mari on noorem **kui** Jüri. | Mari is younger **than** Jüri. |
| Õpilased teavad, **et** kevadel tulevad eksamid. | The students know **that** there will be exams in spring. |

Conjunctions can also form **compound constructions**: nii ... kui ka *both ... and*, ei ... ega *neither ... nor*, kas ... või *either ... or*, mitte ainult ... vaid ka *not only ... but also*, etc.

| Eesti keeles | In English |
|---|---|
| Unustasin koju **nii** passi **kui ka** juhiload. | I forgot **both** the passport **and** the driving licence at home. |
| Haigel ei valutanud **mitte ainult** kurk, **vaid ka** pea. | The patient had **not only** a sore throat **but also** a headache. |

## 4. Interjektsioon e hüüdsõna · Interjections

Interjections are **uninflected** words that:

1. express the speaker's feelings, emotions, and attitude — oi *ah*, ai *ouch*, oh *oh*, oo *wow*, ennäe *lo and behold*, hurraa *hurrah*.

   | Eesti keeles | In English |
   |---|---|
   | Oh häda! | alas! |
   | Ennäe, missugune üllatus! | Lo and behold, what a surprise! |

2. express the speaker's wish to make somebody act (in a certain way) — kuss *hush*, kõtt *shoo*, nõõ *gee up*, äss *attack (urging a dog to attack)*, säh *there*.

3. are used as discourse markers — halloo *hello*, noh *well*, jah *yes*, ah nii ~ ah soo *I see*, nii-nii *I see*.

4. imitate nature and animal sounds — auh *woof*, miäu *miaow*, röh *oink*, prääks *quack*, põmm *boom*, plärts *splash*, etc.
