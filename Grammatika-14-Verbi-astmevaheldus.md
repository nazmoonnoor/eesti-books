# 14 · Verbi astmevaheldus. Gradation of Verbs

Chapter 14 of the *Eesti keele grammatika* reference, **book pages 52–53**. Not every verb stem grades — how to test whether one does, and the two kinds of alternation: quantity (stem-sound length) and quality (consonant/​s presence, lost either through assimilation or outright loss).

> **SG** = strong grade, **WG** = weak grade. Word forms are given as **-ma infinitive : -da infinitive : 1st person present indicative**.

## 1. Astmevahelduse tuvastamine · Determining Gradation

Gradation does **not** occur in all verb stems.

To determine whether a verb is gradational or not, **three forms** must be compared: the **-ma infinitive**, the **-da infinitive**, and the **1st person present indicative**.

The **-ma infinitive is always in the strong grade**. The -da infinitive and the 1st person present indicative can be either in the weak or in the strong grade.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| TA (tugev aste) = SG: õppima, SG: õppida, NA (nõrk aste) = WG: õpin | study |
| SG: hüppama, WG: hüpata, SG: hüppan | jump |

---

## 2. Kvantiteedivaheldus · Quantity Alternation

Quantity alternation occurs when the **length of the stem sounds** changes. Many verbs with quantity alternation do **not** reveal the quantity change in their spelling.

**SG : SG : WG**

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| laulma : laulda : laulan | to sing |
| sammuma : sammuda : sammun | to step |
| rääkima : rääkida : räägin | to talk |

**SG : WG : SG**

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| arvama : arvata : arvan | to think |
| haarama : haarata : haaran | to grab |
| ruttama : rutata : ruttan | to hurry |
| kuulma : kuulda : kuulen | to hear |
| kallama : kallata : kallan | to pour |

In the case of some stem constructions, **stops are spelt with double letters** (kk, pp, tt) in the strong grade and single letters (k, p, t) in the weak grade, **or** with k, p, t in the strong grade and with g, b, d in the weak grade.

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| uppuma : uppuda : upun | to be drowned |
| tõlkima : tõlkida : tõlgin | to translate |
| hakkama : hakata : hakkan | to begin |
| vaatama : vaadata : vaatan | to look |

---

## 3. Kvaliteedivaheldus: assimilatsioon · Quality Alternation: Assimilation

**Quality alternation** occurs if stop consonants or **s** are present in the strong grade and **absent** in the weak grade.

**Assimilation** occurs where a stop consonant or **s** becomes the **same sound** as the preceding voiced consonant. (SG: d, b → WG: rr, nn, ll, mm)

**SG : SG : WG**

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| kandma : kanda : kannan | to wear |
| murdma : murda : murran | to break |
| pilduma : pilduda : pillun | to throw, to hurl |

**SG : WG : SG**

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| õmblema : õmmelda : õmblen | to sew |
| lendama : lennata : lendan | to fly |
| kuldama : kullata : kuldan | to gild |
| kordama : korrata : kordan | to repeat |

Compare also andma : anda : annan *to give* (SG:SG:WG) and tõmbama : tõmmata : tõmban *to pull* (SG:WG:SG) — assimilation to a double sonorant, the same pattern as above.

---

## 4. Kvaliteedivaheldus: kadu · Quality Alternation: Loss of Sounds

In the case of **loss**, the k, t, b, d, g sounds in the weak stem either:

**1) disappear:**

**SG : SG : WG**

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| lõhkuma : lõhkuda : lõhun | to break |
| tohtima : tohtida : tohin | may |
| kaduma : kaduda : kaon | to disappear |
| tegema : teha : teen | to do |
| lugema : lugeda : loen | to read |

**SG : WG : SG**

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| kõhklema : kõhelda : kõhklen | to hesitate |
| kohtlema : kohelda : kohtlen | to treat |
| vaidlema : vaielda : vaidlen | to argue |
| algama : alata : algan | to begin |
| viskama : visata : viskan | to throw |

**2) are replaced by other sounds (v, j):**

| Sõna | English |
|---|---|
| sadama : sadada : sajab | to be raining |
| kaebama : kaevata : kaeban | to complain |
| hülgama : hüljata : hülgan | to desert |
